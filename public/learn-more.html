What's wrong with them?

Have you ever heard the phrase 'there are two sides to every story'? Yea.

If journalists rely on just one source, it can raise issues of accuracy, reliability and balance.

Is it ever ok to publish a story with just one source?

Of course. Some good examples are news stories about speeches, possibly studies or profile on individuals.

News organisations might also have a policy that single sources can be used with the approval of an editor who is satisfied that the source is authoritative or that the story should be be an exception.

Confession

In modern newsrooms, there is immense pressure to produce a large quantity of stories quickly. The makes of this app have published single source stories. This project is about recognising that those stories would have been much better - the reader would have been more informed, and our publication more trusted and balanced - if those stories had been more thorough and balanced.

What does this app do?

This app reveals stories that have drawn on or accredited one source (with a margin of error yet to be determined). It gives the reader the opportunity to identify these stories and assess whether they think the story is informative and fair.

What does this app not do?

This app does not detect bias in story. For example a story might have three sources, but they all share the same opinion. Not does it necessarily impale all stories that have only used one source.
